EARLY MORNINGS

A balance of health and indulgence in more ways than one, Fourth Chapter is a local institution that has helped shape the iconic brunch culture that now defines the city.
With a menu that brings inspired local produce to an urban setting, Abacus sources most ingredients from the neighbouring Prahran market — and don’t go past the bread baked in-house.
A self-described staples and specialty grocer, Morning Market features exactly that; the expected made extraordinary. Think: sandwiches, salads, and baked goods, plus plenty of local produce.
A local favourite for all the right reasons; Moby is a place to see and be seen by all the right people. Frequent for your early morning coffee run or casual brunch date — here, the menu caters to all.

HOURS OF LEISURE

The ultimate cultural hub, Hawksburn Village is an epicentre of local activity. Home to Australian designer boutiques, bakeries and cafes, this strip of shops represents so much more than the limited real estate it operates within.
A storefront as discreet as the dainty jewellery its renowned for, Armadale’s Sarah and Sebastian is not only Melbourne’s flagship store, but home to some of the most coveted designs in the country.
A first-class venue for tennis, squash and equipped with a full gym, lap pool and spa; Kooyong is the place to play with local elites. There’s a reason it’s internationally referred to as ‘The home of Australian tennis’.
Studio Pilates is the place to flex your wellness. A welcoming presence and an energetic atmosphere, here, your morning routine will solidify as your muscles do.

DINNER’S SERVED

Whether you choose the contemporary sensory or insight menus, your impression will be set by the chef personally announcing each course as it arrives — a standard only exceeded by the quality that echoes every bite.
This is Japanese cuisine at its most precise. It’s food that looks as good as it tastes in an equally enchanting atmosphere, and a reason to put this as a must on your local list.
Casual, authentic, and completely delicious — Neighbourhood has made a name for its Neapolitan bases topped with fresh buffalo mozzarella. Come for the food, stay for the fairy-light adorned outdoor terrace.
A fine dining institution as authentic as the French cuisine it serves, Chez Bob is a favourite amongst well connected locals. We favour the classics but if you’re not fluent in French flavours, we simply recommend you try it all.

AFTER DARK

With a rotating menu of 20 wines by the glass or an seemingly endless off-the-shelf policy, this local favourite injects a taste of luxury into its effortlessly casual existence.
A tongue-in-cheek take on Parisian nightlife, Bouzy Bar à Vins emulates the luxurious culture with a playful tone that sets the mood for a night of indulgence; good company and good wine included.
From the brick and burgundy interior to wines sourced directly from Bourgogne, Auterra is a taste of new in more ways than one. Expect eccentric bite-sized dishes like you haven’t tasted before.
More than your average bar and bottle shop, Milton Wine Shop is dedicated to its craft, and even more so, the craft of the winemakers it represents. Plus, the ‘no wine snobbery’ policy sets the tone.

For when you need eight days in a week

Combining the buzz of South Yarra, with the village of Toorak and the convenience of Hawksburn, this is a lifestyle that will have you trading snoozes for sneakers on Sunday — and every day.
